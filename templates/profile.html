What brought me to coding

Before I began coding I was working a fairly standard admin job for Cardiff Council. Administration, believe it or not, was not my childhood dream but I did get along well with the people I worked with. Then, out of the blue, the world closed down amidst a global pandemic. Homeworking became standard. Deprived of the people I'd become close to, with just a mundane job for company, I began to explore other options.

I weighed up how I'd like to work, which is in a skill based position where I'm able to continuously develop and am likely to be rewarded for it. I considered what I find engaging; I've spent my life on computers dipping into professional software like Photoshop and Illustrator, more thoroughly learning music production programs starting with Fruity Loops and then continuing with Ableton Live for the past 7 years or so, and occasionally dabbling in the video editing software Adobe Premiere primarily for my media studies A-level and then more recently making videos for friend and family birthdays. I'd say there are two main benefits that I've taken from these hobbies, the first is a familiarity with how professional software tends to function at the user experience level and the second is a knack for independent learning, troubleshooting and problem solving. I also wanted to be sure my chosen career would be somewhat future proof as I was, and am, planning on dedicating my foreseeable future to developing this skill set.

My first instinct was to study for a qualification alongside my job. One evening while getting a lift home from a family friend with a background in software development I asked for some advice, he said that in his opinion it's much better to get experience than a qualification if possible. A few weeks later he got in contact and suggested that I work for him developing an app. Of course I jumped at the opporunity.

The upsides have been tremendous; being able to dedciate all of my working hours to developing an app single-handedly from start to finish and all of the experience that's gone along with that, being able to test and play and develop my skills independently (meaning no shortcuts, that every problem is thought through to its solution and absorbed thoroughly), and understanding not only the task that I was working on at the time but necessarily how it would fit into the app as a whole.
